At its current state, farming is the simplest, easiest, and most consistent job in Crown City. Consequently, it is also the job with the least payout. The farming job consists of harvesting wheat for the city and (if you are a good member of the community) replanting the wheat with your watering can for future farmers to harvest.

How to Farm

Before you can begin harvesting wheat you will need a hoe, which can be purchased from the tools merchant in the market. You also are given a free hoe upon entering the city for the first time. The material of the hoe doesn't make any difference; the wooden hoe is just as good as the iron hoe. For replanting you need a watering can, which can be obtained for free on-site by right-clicking the blue glass block next to the windmill.

The farming job takes place in the fields surrounding the flour mill. The easiest way to access it is to take the coach to the windmill in North Pass.

To harvest wheat, simply right-click the wheat with your hoe. The wheat you harvest is automatically collected by your bag of holding and replaced with coins as thanks for your contribution to the city's food supplies. Harvesting uses about 1 point of energy per 3 uses of the hoe, and pays 7 copper for each stock of wheat harvested.

Once you are finished harvesting the wheat, it is important to replant in the places where you harvested. To do this, use your watering can to pour enchanted seed-be-grow fluid on farmland. Wheat will grow in a 3x3 square around where you pour the water. Continue pouring water until the wheat is fully grown. Watering the field requires no energy, but gives no payout and can only be done in the fields by the windmill because the enchantment on the watering can wears off.

I suggest the farming job to anyone who wants to grind out their energy without the risk of the mining or beekeeping jobs and without having to pay attention to minigames like in smithing and cooking.

Changes to Farming

  • Using the watering can originally used energy. This was changed so you could water the farm for free without using energy.
  • When the city first opened, there were trapdoor blocks over the irrigation puddles. This enabled an exploit where you could harvest wheat without destroying the block, allowing you to harvest much faster and not have to replant.
