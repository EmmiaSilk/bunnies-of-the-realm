Right now, Beekeeping is the quickest way to earn money. It has the best money-per-enegy ratio of all the jobs, and with the help of honeycombs it can be one of the fastest jobs. Beekeeping consists of scooping up honey from the hive and delivering them to the Human Wares merchant. The only catch is the bees, which make it one of the riskiest jobs in the Crown City, next to mining.

How to Harvest Honey

To scoop up honey from the hive, all you need is an empty bottle. You can buy these bottles at the Human Wares Merchant in the market or reuse bottles that previously contained drinks. You should also have food on-hand to recuperate from the bees' poison. You can make the beekeeping job easier with the help of a Honeycomb Apple, which can be obtained from the Glade Fruit Basket contract.

The beekeeping job takes place in the beehive in Treeven's Canopy. The easiest way to access it is by taking the coach to the great tree in the hallowed glade. Head into the tree and find the podzol dance pad. You will know you have found it when purple sparkles begin to swirl around you. Press shift to dance on the dance pad and be whisked away to the canopy. You should be able to see the beehive on one of the branches. Climb into the branch find a hole that leads to the beehive.

To scoop up honey, hop down into the opening of the hive and use an empty bottle on the honey blocks inside the tree. Be careful not to get cornered by bees, as they can knock you deeper into the hive and send you to an early grave. If you're lucky, you may be able to harvest honey without getting stung with their venomous stingers. Once you are done scooping honey, (usually you can only safely scoop up one bottle of honey per go), jump down to the safety of water down below. Filling a bottle of honey uses up 3 points of energy, and one honey bottle can be sold to the Human Wares Merchant for 2 silver a piece.

You can speed up the process of harvesting honey with the help of a Honeycomb. Eat the honeycomb before jumping into the hive. This allows you to disguise yourself as a bee and infiltrate the hive. The disguise lasts for 30 seconds, and prevents bees from noticing you while disguised. You can still be attacked by bees that noticed you before you ate the Honeycomb, so keep your guard up. The cooldown on scooping honey is 3 seconds, so you should be able to safely fill 10 honey bottles before the disguise wears off. After using the honeycomb, you have to wait 4 minutes before you can use another one.

I recommend beekeeping to anyone with an extra honeycomb lying around. I also recommend the job to anyone who values efficient energy usage or wants to take a risk.

Changes to Beekeeping

  • Filling a bottle with honey originally used 1 stamina on release. This was changed to 3 stamina with the introduction of honeycombs.
  • Honeycombs originally had a cooldown of 2 minutes. This was very quickly changed to 4 minutes for balance purposes.
